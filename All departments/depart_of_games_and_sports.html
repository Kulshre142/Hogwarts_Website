 Department  of  Magical  Games  and  Sports



The Department of Magical Games and Sports was a department of the Ministry of Magic. It was charged with the regulation of all sport-related events in the Wizarding world. It was roughly the equivalent to the real-life British Department of Culture, Media and Sport.The International Statute of Wizarding Secrecy was signed in 1692, and it made each individual Ministry of Magic responsible for the consequences of all magical sports played within their countries. The Ministry started passing legislation pertaining to Quidditch in 1750 .

Head's of Department

Hamish MacFarlan was a British wizard who was a Quidditch player for the Montrose Magpies, captaining the side from 1957 to 1968. After retiring from the game, MacFarlan became the head of the Department of Magical Games and Sports.

Hamish MacFarlan

Ludovic "Ludo" Bagman was an English wizard and Ministry of Magic employee. During his career at the Ministry, Ludo rose to the post of Head of the Department of Magical Games and Sports.

Ludovic "Ludo" Bagman

Bertha Jorkins was a witch and Ministry of Magic employee who worked in the Department of Magical Games and Sports before her death.
